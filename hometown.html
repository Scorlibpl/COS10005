About Tanjungpinang

Tanjung Pinang is the capital of the Riau Islands Indonesian province. It is the second largest city in the province, with an estimated population of 209,280 citizens. Tanjung Pinang is a historic city in the Malay world. Tanjung Pinang is a historic Malay culture city which serves as the capital of the Sultanate of Johor and Riau-Lingga. Tanjung Pinang, whose name is derived from the position of a beech tree jutting into the sea, trees in the Cape, which is a guide for travellers going to Bintan River. The Tanjung Pinang River is the gateway to Bintan, where the Bentan Empire was based in what is now the Bukit Batu subdivision.
